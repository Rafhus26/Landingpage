What Is an FHA Loan?

Flexible Financing for Massachusetts Homebuyers

An FHA loan is a government-backed mortgage insured by the Federal Housing Administration — a division of the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD). Because the federal government insures the loan, lenders can offer more lenient qualification requirements compared to conventional mortgages.

FHA loans are especially popular with first-time homebuyers in Massachusetts because they require only 3.5% down with a credit score of 580 or higher. Even borrowers with scores between 500 and 579 may qualify with a 10% down payment. Gift funds from family members are allowed for the entire down payment and closing costs.

In Massachusetts, FHA loan limits vary by county. For 2024, the FHA loan limit for Worcester County and most of MA is $498,257 for a single-family home — high enough to cover a wide range of properties throughout the state.

Why Choose FHA

Key Benefits of an FHA Loan

FHA loans remove many of the barriers that prevent qualified buyers from entering the market.

💵
Low Down Payment

Put down as little as 3.5% of the purchase price — on a $400,000 home, that's just $14,000. Down payment funds can come from savings, a gift, or down payment assistance programs.

📋
Flexible Credit Guidelines

FHA loans accept credit scores as low as 580 — and sometimes 500 with a larger down payment. Recent late payments, collections, or past bankruptcy are more forgiving than conventional guidelines.

🎁
Gift Funds Allowed

Your entire down payment and closing costs can be a gift from a family member, employer, or nonprofit organization. No portion needs to come from your own funds.

📈
Competitive Interest Rates

Because FHA loans are government-insured, lenders often offer competitive rates even to borrowers with lower credit scores — sometimes lower than comparable conventional loans.

🏠
Streamline Refinance Option

Once you have an FHA loan, you may qualify for an FHA Streamline Refinance — a simplified process to lower your rate with minimal documentation and no appraisal in many cases.

👥
Multi-Unit Properties

FHA loans can be used to purchase 2-, 3-, or 4-unit properties as long as you live in one unit. This strategy lets you offset your mortgage with rental income from the other units.

Do You Qualify?

FHA Loan Requirements in Massachusetts

FHA guidelines are flexible, but there are still minimum requirements. Here's what you need to qualify.

Credit & Income
  • ✓580+ credit score for 3.5% down; 500–579 for 10% down
  • ✓Stable employment history — typically 2 years with the same employer or in the same field
  • ✓Debt-to-income ratio (DTI) generally under 43% (up to 50% with compensating factors)
  • ✓No income limits — FHA is available at any income level
  • ✓Self-employed borrowers must show 2 years of tax returns
Property & Down Payment
  • ✓Property must be your primary residence (no vacation homes or investment-only properties)
  • ✓FHA appraisal required — property must meet HUD minimum property standards
  • ✓Loan amount must be within FHA county limits ($498,257 for most of MA in 2024)
  • ✓Down payment of 3.5% (580+ score) or 10% (500–579 score)
  • ✓Mortgage Insurance Premium (MIP) required for the life of the loan if down payment < 10%
Common Questions

FHA Loan FAQ

FHA loans require two types of mortgage insurance: an upfront MIP of 1.75% of the loan amount (paid at closing or rolled into the loan), and an annual MIP paid monthly. If your down payment is less than 10%, annual MIP stays for the life of the loan. If you put down 10% or more, MIP cancels after 11 years. Unlike PMI on conventional loans, FHA MIP cannot be canceled simply by reaching 20% equity — you would need to refinance into a conventional loan to eliminate it once you have enough equity.
Yes, FHA loans are one of the most forgiving options after a major credit event. After a Chapter 7 bankruptcy, you typically need to wait 2 years. After a Chapter 13 bankruptcy, you may qualify in as little as 1 year with court approval and a good payment record. After a foreclosure, the standard waiting period is 3 years — shorter than the 7-year wait typically required for a conventional loan.
Absolutely. FHA loans allow 100% of the down payment to come from a gift from a family member, close friend, employer, or eligible nonprofit. The donor will need to provide a gift letter stating the funds are a gift and not a loan, along with documentation of the transfer. This is one of the most significant advantages of FHA over many conventional loan programs.
The main trade-off is mortgage insurance. FHA loans have lower down payment and credit requirements but require MIP for at least 11 years (or the life of the loan). Conventional loans with 20%+ down have no mortgage insurance, and PMI on conventional loans can be removed once you reach 20% equity. For buyers with strong credit (700+) and at least 5% down, a conventional loan may be more cost-effective long-term. For buyers with lower credit scores or limited savings, FHA is often the better path.
