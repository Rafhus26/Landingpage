What Is a VA Loan?

A Mortgage Benefit Earned Through Service

A VA loan is a mortgage guaranteed by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs. It's one of the only loan programs that allows eligible borrowers to purchase a home with absolutely no down payment and no monthly mortgage insurance — two features that can save tens of thousands of dollars over the life of a loan.

In Massachusetts, VA loans are available to veterans, active-duty service members, National Guard and Reserve members who have met minimum service requirements, and eligible surviving spouses. There is no VA loan limit for borrowers with full VA entitlement — meaning you can finance a home at any price point with $0 down.

VA loans do require a one-time VA funding fee, which can be financed into the loan. Some veterans — including those with a service-connected disability — are exempt from this fee entirely.

Why Choose VA

Key Benefits of a VA Loan

VA loans offer benefits no other loan program can match — built specifically to honor your service.

💵
Zero Down Payment

Purchase a home in Massachusetts without putting a single dollar down. On a $500,000 home, that's $100,000 you keep in your pocket compared to a 20% down conventional loan.

🔒
No Mortgage Insurance

Unlike FHA and conventional loans with less than 20% down, VA loans never require monthly mortgage insurance. This alone can save $200–$400+ per month on a typical Massachusetts home.

📈
Competitive Rates

VA loans typically carry interest rates 0.25%–0.5% lower than conventional loans. Over a 30-year loan, this translates to tens of thousands of dollars in savings.

🔄
Reusable Benefit

Your VA entitlement doesn't expire and can be used multiple times. Even if you currently have an existing VA loan, you may be able to use remaining or restored entitlement to purchase again.

🌟
Flexible Credit Guidelines

The VA doesn't set a minimum credit score, though most lenders require 580–620. VA loans are more forgiving of past credit challenges than conventional guidelines.

🏠
VA IRRRL Refinance

Already have a VA loan? The Interest Rate Reduction Refinance Loan (IRRRL) lets you lower your rate with minimal paperwork and no out-of-pocket costs in many cases.

Who Qualifies?

VA Loan Eligibility Requirements

Eligibility is based on military service history. Here's what qualifies you for a VA loan in Massachusetts.

Service Requirements
  • ✓Active-duty service member with 90+ consecutive days of service
  • ✓Veteran discharged under conditions other than dishonorable after 90+ days (wartime) or 181+ days (peacetime)
  • ✓National Guard or Reserve member with 6+ years of service
  • ✓Eligible surviving spouse of a veteran who died in service or from a service-connected disability
Loan & Property Requirements
  • ✓Certificate of Eligibility (COE) — I can pull this directly with your consent
  • ✓Property must be your primary residence
  • ✓VA appraisal required — property must meet VA Minimum Property Requirements (MPRs)
  • ✓Sufficient residual income — VA evaluates money left over after all monthly obligations
  • ✓Credit score typically 580+ (lender overlay may vary)
Common Questions

VA Loan FAQ

The VA funding fee is a one-time fee paid to the VA that helps keep the program running for future generations. For a first-time VA loan with $0 down, the fee is 2.15% of the loan amount (as of 2024). It can be financed into the loan so you pay nothing at closing. Veterans with a service-connected disability rating of 10% or more are completely exempt from the funding fee. Surviving spouses of veterans who died in service or from a service-connected disability are also exempt.

You'll need your DD-214 (discharge papers) if you're a veteran, or your latest statement of service if you're currently active duty.

If you still own the property but have remaining entitlement, you may be able to use that bonus entitlement to purchase a second home — even without selling the first. This is a nuanced area and I'd love to walk you through your specific situation.
